Food as Information

Why do some foods make you feel energized while others leave you crashing an hour later?

The Science:

Every bite you take sends signals to your cellsâ€”food is information, not just fuel. Your HbA1c and triglyceride levels directly reflect your dietary choices over the past 2-3 months. Foods high in refined sugars and processed carbs create glucose spikes that, over time, lead to insulin resistance and elevated HbA1c.

Meanwhile, whole foods rich in fiber, healthy fats, and lean proteins help stabilize blood sugar and lower triglycerides. Research shows that people following anti-inflammatory eating patterns have 30% lower risk of metabolic dysfunction and maintain healthier biomarker profiles throughout life.

Your Action Step:

Add one serving of colorful vegetables to your next meal, as your nutrition plan allows. The phytonutrients that create vibrant colors also fight inflammation and support optimal metabolic function.
